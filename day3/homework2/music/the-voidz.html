The Voidz - New album

Julian Casablancas shares details of his new album with band the Voidz

A year since the Strokes' last LP and almost five years since his solo debut, Julian Casablancas has shed some light on his forthcoming project with his new band, the Voidz. Entitled, Can I VHS you? and shot like a shaky handheld You've Been Framed clip – with not an inebriated granny at a wedding in sight, unfortunately – the video features an interview with the Strokes frontman in the front seat of a Monte Carlo, as well as rehearsal footage from their first show in Washington and bursts of the band's material. So far it sounds noisier, wirier and tuned to the dirgey punk rock of 1970s New York. "There's a lot of in-between areas in music that haven't been exploited," the singer begins, "and it's trying to become that gap."

What will the album sound like?

"Jazz and punk – amazing melodic things and flavours" "Music of our time" "World sounding with a lot of power"

What does it all mean?

"[It's] a protest record. I try and keep it subtle and universal but it is pretty sad I guess, that there seems to be no way around corruption being king now and forever and always. "I felt like the best cool things are never cool in their time. It always takes years. It almost has a political parallel, like truth in politics: 30 years later people will say 'yeah slavery is terrible' but when it's happening in their day it's just clueless."

No further details regarding the album are available yet. Julian Casablancas will be performing both with the Voidz and the Strokes at the Governors Ball music festival in New York City in June.
